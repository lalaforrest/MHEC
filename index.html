The Problem

Founders face a mental health crisis hiding in plain sight

01

Invisible Suffering

Founders are disproportionately affected by anxiety, depression, and burnout — yet the culture of entrepreneurship treats these as weaknesses to be hidden rather than conditions to be treated. Entrepreneurs report depression at nearly three times the rate of the general population.¹

02

No Specialized Care

General outpatient psychiatry is not designed for the unique pressures of building a company — the fundraising cycles, the isolation of leadership, the existential stakes, and the identity fusion between founder and venture. Research shows that entrepreneurs present with distinct psychiatric profiles requiring specialized clinical approaches.²

03

Access Gaps

Early-stage founders often lack employer-sponsored insurance, financial stability, or time. The mental health system was not built for them — and most clinicians have no framework for their lived experience. Only 30% of entrepreneurs report feeling comfortable discussing mental health challenges with peers, and fewer still seek formal care.¹

Our Mission

Psychiatric care that understands the founder experience

MHEC exists to close the gap between the mental health needs of entrepreneurs and the care available to them. We offer evidence-based psychiatric services delivered by clinicians who understand the startup ecosystem — its rhythms, its pressures, and its particular demands on the human mind.

We believe that founders who are mentally well build better companies, lead more humanely, and create more durable value. Mental health is not a distraction from the work — it is foundational to it.

Principle 01

Grounded in evidence

Every aspect of care is rooted in psychiatric best practice, delivered under rigorous clinical supervision — because founders deserve the same standard of care as anyone else.

Principle 02

Built on relationship

Continuity matters. You will see the same clinician over time — someone who knows your history, understands your context, and is genuinely invested in your wellbeing.

Principle 03

Fluent in your world

Our clinicians understand the entrepreneurship ecosystem — the fundraising cycles, the isolation of leadership, the weight of building something from nothing.

Principle 04

Accessible by design

Sliding scale and sliding-fee options ensure that financial constraints do not stand between a founder and the care they need.

What We Offer

Clinical and supportive services for the founder behind the venture

Clinical

Psychiatric Evaluation & Diagnosis

Comprehensive psychiatric assessment tailored to founders and entrepreneurs, with an understanding of how the startup environment intersects with mental health presentation and history.

Clinical

Medication Management

Ongoing psychiatric medication management as part of a longitudinal care relationship — not a one-time prescription. We monitor, adjust, and support over time.

Wellness

Founder Wellness Programming

Group workshops, peer support, and community programming designed specifically for the entrepreneurship ecosystem — addressing burnout, resilience, and leadership wellbeing. Programming extends into executive coaching, organizational leadership development, and the interpersonal and relational dynamics that shape how founders lead teams, navigate co-founder relationships, and build organizational culture.

Crisis

Crisis Triage & Escalation

Structured crisis protocols and clear escalation pathways — so that founders know they have a clinical home with a clear plan when things get hard, not just a voicemail box.

About the Founder

Lala Forrest, MD

Founder & Physician  ·  Psychiatry Resident

Dr. Lala Forrest is a psychiatry resident and the founder of MHEC. She is developing this clinic at the intersection of academic medicine and the entrepreneurship ecosystem, with a deep commitment to making specialized psychiatric care accessible to founders and innovation leaders who have historically been underserved by the mental health system.

Her vision for MHEC grows from a conviction that the pressures unique to entrepreneurship — the isolation of leadership, the existential stakes, the relentless performance demands — require clinicians who genuinely understand that world, not just clinicians who see it from the outside. She is building MHEC to be that place.
